A trapezoid has bases of lengths \(10 cm\) and \(15 cm\), and a height of \(8 cm\). If the longer base is increased by \(25%\) and the height is decreased by \(20%\), what is the new area of the trapezoid?

\(92 cm^{2}\)

\(108 cm^{2}\)

\(120 cm^{2}\)

\(132 cm^{2}\)

The area of a trapezoid is given by the formula \( \frac{1}{2}(b_1 + b_2)h \), where \( b_1 \) and \( b_2 \) are the lengths of the bases, and \( h \) is the height.
Initially, the area is \( \frac{1}{2}(10 + 15) \times 8 = 100 \) \(cm^{2}\).
Now, increasing the longer base by \(25%\) makes it \( 15 \times 1.25 = 18.75 \) \(cm\), and decreasing the height by \(20%\) makes it \( 8 \times 0.8 = 6.4 \) \(cm\).
The new area is \( \frac{1}{2}(10 + 18.75) \times 6.4 = 92 \) \(cm^{2}\),
which corresponds to options A.
Therefore, the correct answer is option A.
