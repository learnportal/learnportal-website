In the given system of equations, \( p \) is a constant. If the system has no solution, what is the value of \( p \)? \[ 4x - 9y = 9y + 5 \] \[ py = 2 + 4x \]

For the system to have no solution, the equations must be parallel, which means their slopes must be the same, but the \(y-\)intercepts must be different. We can rewrite the first equation in slope-intercept form \((y = mx + b)\) as follows: \[ 4x - 9y = 9y + 5 \] \[ -18y = -4x + 5 \] \[ y = \frac{4}{18}x - \frac{5}{18} \] The slope of the first equation is \( \frac{2}{9} \). For the second equation: \[ py = 2 + 4x \] \[ y = \frac{4}{p}x + \frac{2}{p} \] For the system to have no solution, the slope of the second equation must also be \( \frac{2}{9} \). \[ \frac{4}{p} = \frac{2}{9} \] Solving for \( p \) gives \( p = 18 \).
