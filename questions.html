Exponents

Introduction

Exponents are a fundamental concept in mathematics that represent repeated multiplication. They are used to simplify and express large numbers or quantities more efficiently.
For example, 2 to the power of 3 can be written as 23 or 2 × 2 × 2.
Exponents can be positive, negative, or fractional. For example, 2 to the power of -3 can be written as
2-3 or 1/8.
Exponents are used in many different areas of mathematics, including algebra, geometry, and trigonometry.
In the context of the SAT math section, a solid understanding of exponents is essential for solving various types of problems efficiently.

Importance in SAT Math

Exponents frequently appear in SAT math questions, particularly in questions related to algebra, equations, and expressions. Understanding how to manipulate and simplify expressions with exponents is crucial for solving these problems effectively.
You may be asked to solve problems involving exponents, such as:

  • Simplify expressions containing exponents or
  • Evaluate exponential expressions or
  • Solve exponential equations.

Here is an example of a digital SAT question involving exponents:
What is the value of 23 + 32?
A. 8
B. 11
C. 12
D. 16
Solution:
23 + 32 = 8 + 9 = 17
Therefore, the answer is D.
