Information and Ideas: Command of Evidence

1

In a recent study, researchers at the Global Climate Institute reported that increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide (CO2) have led to significant changes in plant photosynthesis processes worldwide. The study analyzed data from over 3,000 plant species and concluded that many plants are now absorbing more CO2 than they did fifty years ago. While this could be initially seen as beneficial for reducing greenhouse gases, the scientists caution that this increased absorption rate is altering the nutritional content of these plants, potentially impacting ecosystems and human food sources.

Which finding, if confirmed by further research, would most directly challenge the implications of the study mentioned above?

Plants in regions with high CO2 levels show no significant change in their absorption rates compared to fifty years ago.

Increased CO2 absorption by plants has led to an overall reduction in global CO2 levels, mitigating some effects of climate change.

The alteration in plant nutritional content due to increased CO2 absorption has been linked to improved health outcomes in several mammalian species.

Many plant species have developed new photosynthetic pathways that allow them to thrive in high CO2 environments without altering their nutritional content.

The correct answer is D. This option directly challenges the implication of the study by suggesting that plants can adapt to increased CO2 levels without negative consequences to their nutritional content. This contrasts with the study's cautionary note regarding the potential impact on ecosystems and human food sources.
