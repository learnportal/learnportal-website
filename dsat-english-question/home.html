Overview

The Reading and Writing section is a critical component of the SAT, assessing your ability to comprehend and analyze written material while showcasing your command of English grammar and usage. The section is divided into two modules, each covering four distinct content domains: Craft and Structure, Information and Ideas, Standard English Conventions, and Expression of Ideas.

Passages and Questions

  • Short reading passages (25-150 words): Covering diverse subjects like literature, history/social studies, science, and humanities.

  • Single multiple-choice question per passage: Testing your comprehension, analysis, and reasoning skills across four key domains:

  • Information and Ideas: Tests comprehension, analysis, and reasoning skills, assessing your ability to locate, interpret, evaluate, and integrate information from various texts and graphics.

  • Craft and Structure: Measures comprehension, vocabulary, analysis, synthesis, and reasoning skills, focusing on your ability to understand and use words and phrases in context.

  • Expression of Ideas: Assesses your ability to revise texts for improved written expression and to meet specific rhetorical goals.

  • Standard English Conventions: Measures your ability to edit text according to core conventions of Standard English sentence structure, usage, and punctuation.
  • Two modules: Each module includes questions from all four domains, starting with easier questions and progressively increasing in difficulty.
